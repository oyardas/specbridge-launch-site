# Synthetic Demo RFP Scenario

Status: Draft
Purpose: Controlled SpecBridge AI demo scenario
Data type: Synthetic / anonymized

## 1. Scenario name

Enterprise Campus Network Modernization RFP

## 2. Buyer profile

A mid-sized enterprise organization is planning to modernize its campus network infrastructure across a main office and two branch locations.

The buyer requires switching, wireless access, network management, support services and documentation.

This is a synthetic scenario. It does not represent any real customer or real tender.

## 3. Business context

The buyer wants to:

- Replace aging access switches
- Improve wireless coverage
- Centralize network management
- Reduce operational troubleshooting effort
- Standardize support and warranty terms
- Avoid vendor lock-in where possible

## 4. Tender sections

The RFP includes the following sections:

- General administrative requirements
- Technical requirements
- Delivery and installation scope
- Warranty and support terms
- Documentation and training
- Commercial submission format
- Compliance table requirement
- Clarification question deadline

## 5. Intentional demo risks

This scenario intentionally includes:

- Incomplete technical requirements
- Ambiguous SLA expectations
- Brand-biased wording
- Missing acceptance criteria
- Commercial submission ambiguity
- Unclear integration expectations
- Support responsibility gaps

## 6. Example clauses

### Clause 1: Submission format

The bidder shall submit a signed and stamped commercial offer together with a technical compliance table and product datasheets.

### Clause 2: Delivery

All equipment shall be delivered within 45 calendar days following purchase order issuance.

### Clause 3: Support

The bidder shall provide minimum three years of warranty and technical support.

### Clause 4: Network management

The proposed solution shall support centralized monitoring, configuration backup, event visibility and user/device inventory.

### Clause 5: Vendor-specific language

The wireless system shall support Magic-style onboarding and single-pane campus control similar to Vendor X architecture.

### Clause 6: Acceptance

The system shall be considered accepted after successful installation and basic connectivity tests.

## 7. Expected SpecBridge AI findings

- Bid/no-bid requires review due to ambiguity
- Technical fit is partially assessable
- Vendor bias should be flagged
- Clarification questions should be generated
- Delivery and acceptance risk should be visible
- Support/SLA scope should require clarification
- Compliance matrix should separate clear, partial and unclear clauses

## 8. Demo use

This scenario is suitable for:

- 5-minute product demo
- Sample report creation
- Vendor bias before/after example
- Pilot discussion
- Sales/presales positioning
