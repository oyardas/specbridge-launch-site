# SpecBridge AI Website Claims Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy controls what the public website may claim about SpecBridge.

The goal is to keep product positioning credible, defensible, and aligned with the long-term strategy.

## 2. Approved Claim Categories

The website may claim that SpecBridge:

- Helps analyze tender documents and technical specifications
- Helps extract and structure requirements
- Helps identify compliance gaps
- Helps detect technical, commercial, administrative, and delivery risks
- Helps generate clarification questions
- Helps support bid/no-bid decision-making
- Helps prepare role-specific reports
- Helps improve tender review consistency
- Helps reduce manual review effort
- Helps organize tender evidence and decision rationale

Use `helps`, `supports`, `highlights`, `organizes`, `structures`, or `improves decision readiness` unless a feature is fully implemented and validated.

## 3. Prohibited Claims

The website must not claim that SpecBridge:

- Guarantees tender wins
- Guarantees full compliance
- Guarantees legal correctness
- Replaces expert review
- Replaces presales, legal, procurement, or executive decision-makers
- Fully automates bid/no-bid decisions
- Produces perfect analysis
- Eliminates all tender risk
- Can safely process all confidential data without qualification
- Has features that are not yet implemented

## 4. AI Capability Claims

Avoid broad AI claims such as:

- Best AI for tenders
- Fully autonomous tender intelligence
- Human-level procurement analysis
- Zero-error AI analysis
- Complete AI proposal generation

Preferred phrasing:

```text
AI-assisted tender intelligence with structured evidence, compliance, risk, and decision-support workflows.
```

## 5. Security and Privacy Claims

Security claims must be specific and accurate.

Allowed if implemented:

- Data masking
- Audit logs
- Private deployment option
- Local processing option
- Role-based access
- User/project separation

Do not claim enterprise-grade security, compliance certification, or full private processing unless implemented and verified.

## 6. Roadmap Claims

Roadmap features must be labeled clearly.

Allowed labels:

- Planned
- In development
- Roadmap
- Enterprise option

Example:

```text
Roadmap: optional private/local processing mode for sensitive tender documents.
```

Do not write:

```text
SpecBridge keeps all tender documents private locally.
```

unless that behavior is implemented and validated.

## 7. Human Review Disclaimer

Where appropriate, include a human review caveat:

```text
SpecBridge supports structured tender review and decision readiness. Final commercial, legal, technical, and procurement decisions should be validated by qualified human reviewers.
```

## 8. Claims Review Checklist

Before publishing a claim, verify:

```text
[ ] Is the claim implemented or clearly labeled as roadmap?
[ ] Is the claim specific rather than vague?
[ ] Does the claim avoid guaranteed outcomes?
[ ] Does the claim preserve human review responsibility?
[ ] Does the claim avoid implying legal/procurement certainty?
[ ] Does the claim support the tender intelligence positioning?
[ ] Would the claim still be defensible in front of an enterprise customer?
```

## 9. Preferred Claim Template

Use this structure for product pages:

```text
SpecBridge helps [audience] [business action] by [structured capability], so they can [decision/workflow outcome].
```

Examples:

```text
SpecBridge helps presales teams identify compliance gaps by extracting requirements and linking findings to source evidence.
```

```text
SpecBridge helps executives review tender risk by turning technical and commercial findings into structured bid/no-bid scorecards.
```

```text
SpecBridge helps sales teams prepare clarification questions by detecting ambiguous, restrictive, or commercially risky requirements.
```
