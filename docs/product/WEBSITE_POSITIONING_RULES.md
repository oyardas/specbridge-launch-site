# SpecBridge AI Website Positioning Rules

## 1. Positioning Rule

The website must not position SpecBridge as a generic AI report generator.

SpecBridge must be positioned as an evidence-based tender intelligence and decision-support platform.

Preferred positioning:

> SpecBridge analyzes tender documents and technical specifications to produce evidence-based compliance matrices, risk registers, clarification questions, bid/no-bid recommendations, and executive-ready decision reports.

## 2. Core Message

The website must communicate that SpecBridge helps organizations:

- Understand tender requirements
- Identify compliance gaps
- Detect technical and commercial risks
- Generate clarification questions
- Evaluate bid/no-bid decisions
- Align sales, presales, technical, and executive teams
- Produce structured reports with evidence
- Reduce manual tender review effort
- Handle sensitive tender data more responsibly than ad-hoc generic AI usage

## 3. Avoided Claims

The website must avoid weak or generic claims such as:

- Upload a document and get an AI summary
- AI writes your reports
- Automatic tender response generator
- Chat with your tender
- One-click proposal writer
- Replace your presales team
- Fully automatic tender decision-making

These claims make the product look easy to replace by generic AI tools.

## 4. Preferred Claims

The website should emphasize:

- Evidence-based tender analysis
- Clause-level traceability
- Compliance intelligence
- Risk visibility
- Bid/no-bid decision support
- Role-specific outputs
- Technical and commercial alignment
- Secure/private document handling
- Repeatable tender workflow
- Vendor-neutral analysis with optional target-brand fit
- Structured decision readiness

## 5. Audience Rule

Website content should address multiple stakeholders.

### Executives

Focus on:

- Faster decisions
- Lower tender risk
- Better go/no-go discipline
- Clear management summaries
- Business impact and accountability

### Sales and Channel Teams

Focus on:

- Opportunity qualification
- Competitive positioning
- Clarification questions
- Action plans
- Partner/distributor alignment

### Presales and Technical Teams

Focus on:

- Requirement extraction
- Compliance matrix
- Technical fit
- Architecture risks
- Product mapping
- Gap identification

### Procurement / Bid Teams

Focus on:

- Structured review
- Auditability
- Document traceability
- Repeatable process
- Human review readiness

## 6. Feature Messaging Rule

Features should be described as decision-support capabilities, not as generic AI functions.

Use this framing:

| Preferred | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Compliance Matrix | AI Table |
| Risk Register | AI Risk Summary |
| Clause-Level Evidence | Document Understanding |
| Bid/No-Bid Scorecard | AI Recommendation |
| Clarification Question Generator | Question Suggestions |
| Tender Strategy Report | Sales Summary |
| Requirement Extraction | AI Parsing |
| Vendor/Product Fit | Brand Matching |

## 7. Differentiation Rule

The website must answer this question clearly:

> Why not just use a generic AI tool?

The answer should be:

> Generic AI can summarize documents, but SpecBridge provides a structured tender intelligence workflow with evidence, compliance tracking, risk classification, bid/no-bid logic, role-specific reports, auditability, and secure handling of sensitive tender data.

## 8. Trust Rule

Website copy must avoid exaggerated promises.

Do not claim:

- Guaranteed tender win
- Full automation without human review
- Perfect compliance detection
- Legal certainty
- Replacement of experts
- Elimination of procurement risk

Use realistic language:

- Supports
- Highlights
- Helps identify
- Provides structured review
- Improves decision readiness
- Reduces manual effort
- Requires human validation for final decisions

## 9. Product Roadmap Language

If roadmap features are mentioned, they must be clearly framed as planned or upcoming.

Do not present unfinished features as already available.

Use labels such as:

- Available
- In development
- Planned
- Roadmap
- Enterprise option

## 10. Website Definition of Done

A website change is complete only if it preserves the strategic position of SpecBridge.

Every major page should reinforce at least one of these ideas:

- Evidence-based analysis
- Compliance intelligence
- Risk visibility
- Bid/no-bid support
- Secure workflow
- Role-specific reporting
- Tender decision support
- Auditability

## 11. Rejection Criteria

Reject or rewrite website copy if it:

- Makes SpecBridge sound like a generic AI summarizer
- Suggests human review is unnecessary
- Overpromises legal/procurement certainty
- Presents unfinished roadmap features as available
- Hides the evidence-based differentiation
- Uses vague AI buzzwords without tender workflow value

## 12. Preferred One-Sentence Product Description

Use this as the default short product description:

> SpecBridge is an evidence-based tender intelligence platform that helps teams analyze requirements, identify compliance gaps, manage risks, generate clarification questions, and prepare bid/no-bid decisions with structured, role-specific reports.
