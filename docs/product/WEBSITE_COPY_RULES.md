# SpecBridge AI Website Copy Rules

## 1. Copy Objective

Website copy must make SpecBridge feel like a serious tender intelligence and decision-support product, not a generic AI assistant.

The copy should be concise, credible, B2B-oriented, and risk-aware.

## 2. Voice and Tone

Preferred tone:

- Professional
- Clear
- Evidence-oriented
- Enterprise-ready
- Realistic
- Decision-focused

Avoid tone that is:

- Overhyped
- Consumer-chatbot-like
- Magical
- Too informal
- Overpromising
- Buzzword-heavy

## 3. Preferred Messaging Pillars

All major copy should map to one or more of the following pillars:

1. Evidence-based tender analysis
2. Compliance matrix and requirement tracking
3. Risk register and risk visibility
4. Clarification question generation
5. Bid/no-bid decision support
6. Role-specific executive, sales, and technical outputs
7. Vendor-neutral analysis with optional target-brand fit
8. Secure and repeatable tender workflow

## 4. Copy Examples

### Strong headline examples

```text
Turn tender documents into structured decision intelligence.
```

```text
Evidence-based compliance, risk, and bid/no-bid support for complex tenders.
```

```text
From technical specifications to executive-ready tender decisions.
```

### Weak headline examples to avoid

```text
Let AI write your tender reports.
```

```text
Upload your tender and get an instant summary.
```

```text
Chat with your RFP.
```

## 5. Feature Copy Rules

Feature descriptions should explain operational value.

### Compliance Matrix

Preferred:

```text
Extract requirements and classify them as compliant, partial, non-compliant, unclear, risky, or requiring clarification.
```

Avoid:

```text
AI creates tables from your tender.
```

### Risk Register

Preferred:

```text
Identify technical, commercial, administrative, SLA, delivery, and vendor-lock risks before committing resources.
```

Avoid:

```text
Get an AI-generated risk summary.
```

### Bid/No-Bid Scorecard

Preferred:

```text
Support go/no-go decisions with structured scoring across technical fit, compliance gaps, delivery feasibility, commercial risk, and strategic value.
```

Avoid:

```text
AI tells you whether to bid.
```

### Clarification Questions

Preferred:

```text
Generate targeted clarification questions for ambiguous, restrictive, incomplete, or commercially risky requirements.
```

Avoid:

```text
AI suggests questions.
```

## 6. CTA Rules

Calls to action should be business-oriented and credible.

Preferred CTAs:

- Request a demo
- Review a sample tender analysis
- See the compliance workflow
- Explore the decision report
- Start structured tender review

Avoid CTAs:

- Let AI do it all
- Generate my tender instantly
- Replace manual review now
- Win more tenders automatically

## 7. Trust and Limitation Language

The website should acknowledge human review where appropriate.

Preferred wording:

```text
SpecBridge supports structured review and decision readiness. Final tender decisions should remain under qualified human review.
```

Avoid wording:

```text
SpecBridge fully automates your tender decisions.
```

## 8. Roadmap Copy Rules

Roadmap features must be clearly labeled.

Allowed labels:

- Available
- In development
- Planned
- Roadmap
- Enterprise option

Example:

```text
Planned: historical tender memory for comparing new opportunities against previous wins, losses, risks, and recurring requirements.
```

Do not imply planned features are live.

## 9. Competitive Differentiation Copy

Preferred explanation:

```text
Generic AI tools can summarize documents. SpecBridge is built for tender workflows: evidence-backed requirements, compliance tracking, risk classification, clarification questions, bid/no-bid logic, and role-specific reports.
```

Avoid attacking competitors directly or claiming universal superiority.

## 10. Copy Review Checklist

Before publishing any website copy, verify:

```text
[ ] It avoids generic AI summarizer positioning.
[ ] It emphasizes tender intelligence and decision support.
[ ] It includes evidence, compliance, risk, or bid/no-bid value.
[ ] It avoids guaranteed outcomes.
[ ] It does not present roadmap features as available.
[ ] It does not suggest expert review is unnecessary.
[ ] It sounds credible to enterprise, vendor, distributor, and presales audiences.
```
