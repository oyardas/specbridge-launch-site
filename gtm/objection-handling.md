# Objection Handling

Status: Draft
Purpose: Early sales and pilot conversations

## Objection 1: We already review tenders manually.

Response:

SpecBridge AI is not intended to replace expert review. It standardizes the first-pass analysis, creates a structured decision surface and helps teams find risks earlier.

## Objection 2: Can it make the final bid/no-bid decision?

Response:

No. It provides advisory decision support. Final bid/no-bid governance remains with the customer organization.

## Objection 3: Can we upload confidential tenders?

Response:

Not before pilot legal, KVKK, retention and deletion rules are agreed. Early pilots should use synthetic, anonymized or redacted documents.

## Objection 4: Is this just an AI summarizer?

Response:

No. The goal is not only summarization. SpecBridge AI structures compliance, gaps, vendor bias, clarification questions, commercial risk, legal/admin risk, delivery risk, SLA/support review and action planning.

## Objection 5: Can it guarantee compliance?

Response:

No. It can support compliance review, but it does not provide final compliance approval or legal advice.

## Objection 6: What makes it useful for management?

Response:

It provides executive-level bid/no-bid signal, risk summary, action plan and decision questions without forcing management to read every clause.

## Objection 7: What makes it useful for presales?

Response:

It accelerates clause review, compliance matrix preparation, gap identification, evidence mapping and clarification question generation.

## Objection 8: What about vendor-biased specifications?

Response:

SpecBridge AI can flag potentially biased wording and suggest neutral rewrite or clarification language to protect bid eligibility.
